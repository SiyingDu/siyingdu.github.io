Context

DevOps knowledge existed but wasn't working. Documentation lived in Google Drive folders nobody opened, policy guides were plain text with no structure, and LMS content was disconnected from where engineers actually worked.

The problem — three layers

Knowledge siloed in Google Drive — no searchable structure, no completion tracking

Policy and setup documents in plain text — no hierarchy, no sequencing, no peer learning

LMS content existed but was disconnected from where engineers worked day-to-day

Three architectural decisions

Decision 1

Platform: Confluence as the hub

Built the knowledge ecosystem where engineers already work — not where the LMS wants them to go. LMS content embedded directly inside Confluence pages.

Decision 2

Experience: Progressive + role-aware

Foundation → Intermediate → Advanced sequencing per pillar. Non-linear navigation so seniors skip to advanced content without a separate document.

Decision 3

Data: Manager-visible tracking

Connected LMS completions to manager-facing progress dashboards — shifting from a passive content library to an accountable development pathway.
