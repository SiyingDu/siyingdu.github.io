Context

HR knowledge existed — but employees couldn't find it. Most defaulted to a Slack message to a human, creating a bottleneck that cost the HR team 6+ hours every week and left global offices without timely support.

The problem — three dimensions

HR partners spent 6+ hrs/week (15% of capacity) answering the same repeatable questions

Employees in global offices had no local HR — waiting across time zones for basic answers

Critical moments — onboarding, compliance deadlines, open enrollment — passed without the right information reaching the right person

In production — Herbie in Slack

30-day new hire check-in — branded Slack message delivered automatically at a lifecycle milestone, with checklist and action button. No manual HR outreach required.

How it works — the hybrid architecture

Herbie uses a two-layer hybrid system — not a single AI model. The design choice was intentional: for high-stakes HR questions, you want the answer to be exactly what HR wrote. For everything else, you need AI flexibility.

The rule-based layer acts as a guardrail. For compliance deadlines, benefits enrollment, and PTO policies — you want the answer to be exactly what HR wrote, with no AI interpretation and no hallucination risk. Glean handles everything else.

No exact FAQ match for this life event. Glean searches HR policies, benefits guides, and Workday documentation to generate a contextual, grounded answer.

Phase 1 design decisions
Decision 1

Content governance — boundaries before capability

Herbie answers

Benefits & Total Rewards HRBP Support L&D Performance Management HR Operations Talent Acquisition

Herbie does not answer

Individual judgment calls — accommodations, disciplinary matters

Legally sensitive queries — employment law interpretation

Personal employee records beyond process confirmation

When Herbie hits a boundary, it routes to the right HR partner — the handoff is designed, not accidental.

Decision 2

Knowledge architecture — one interface, multiple sources

Google Drive

HR-curated FAQ pairs migrated into Workato. Re-indexing triggered on update — answers stay current, not static.

LMS content

Surfaces modules directly via Glean — not just a description, the actual training resource at point of need.

Process docs

Curated before ingestion by HR partners — cleaning the source rather than relying on AI to compensate.

Decision 3

Conversational UX — answer quality and guided navigation

2
