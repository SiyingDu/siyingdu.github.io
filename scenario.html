eLearning Design Using Design Thinking

Background

In the travel industry, agents in customer service centers must navigate complex systems and software, such as Global Distribution Systems (GDS) and Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platforms, while assisting customers. This eLearning solution is designed as a self-paced, web-based training that utilizes simulations, allowing agents to practice using the software during the learning process, bridging the gap between training and real-world application.

Learning Needs Design Analysis & Insight

By leveraging design thinking, we conducted interviews with subject matter experts and user research to gain a deeper understanding of the agents' needs and pain points. From this analysis, four key insights emerged:

  • Insight 1: Agents struggle to determine when to use specific functions within the system.
  • Insight 2: Many agents are non-native English speakers, making abstract concepts difficult to grasp.
  • Insight 3: The current mobile experience is not user-friendly, limiting agents' ability to learn on the go.
  • Insight 4: Technically challenged agents require a simpler, more intuitive interface to feel confident in their software interactions.

By empathizing with these learners, we were able to tailor the design to address these core challenges.

Learning Theory Applied

Cognitive Load Theory (John Sweller) informs our approach by emphasizing that learning occurs most effectively when cognitive overload is minimized. To facilitate schema acquisition, the eLearning course is designed to optimize working memory, ensuring that agents can process and internalize the instructional material without feeling overwhelmed. By focusing on reducing the mental burden, we enable the agents to transfer their knowledge from short-term to long-term memory more effectively.

Design Process

Guided by design thinking and cognitive load principles, the course was designed to meet the needs of diverse learners while addressing the challenges identified in the analysis. The key steps in our design process included:

  • Modular Re-structuring: The entire learning experience was restructured to provide clear, digestible modules that introduce the various functions and processes of the systems. Each module focuses on specific tasks, making it easier for learners to follow and apply their knowledge in real-life scenarios.
  • Scenario-based Simulations: Interactive simulations were incorporated to allow agents to practice real-world tasks. This hands-on experience helps reinforce learning by giving agents a safe environment to practice and make mistakes without real-world consequences.
  • Use of Animation: Animations were introduced to illustrate complex, abstract concepts that are difficult to explain through text alone. This visual support enhances comprehension and provides an alternative means of explanation for difficult concepts.
  • Accessibility through Captions: To support non-native English speakers and visual learners, captions and transcripts were added. This ensures that learners can follow along at their own pace, enhancing comprehension and accessibility for diverse learner profiles.
  • Mobile Optimization: The course was redesigned to be fully mobile-responsive, ensuring that agents can access and use the training modules on the go, with a focus on making navigation intuitive and seamless.

By focusing on user needs through design thinking, we were able to create an eLearning experience that is not only informative but also intuitive, accessible, and engaging. The result is a solution that reduces cognitive load, provides clear guidance on complex systems, and supports agents in improving their performance in the customer service environment.
